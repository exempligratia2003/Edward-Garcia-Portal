How my Music Journey Went

I'm a Philippines-based lo-fi hip-hop song producer. In 2018, while I was a high school student in my tenth year, I started my path into producing music. I first dabbled in various kinds of EDM genres, including future house, tropical house, future bounce, and future bass. But that December, I was introduced to lo-fi hip-hop by a classmate. I was immediately drawn to this genre because of its soothing, soulful feelings, and it has since taken focal point in my life.

I began releasing official beats on streaming platforms in 2020. In the summer of that year, I hit a growing milestone—getting 1,000 listens on my first album, "24 Hours." It was at this moment that I started to produce music more consistently. In an attempt to get their support, I created a small web presence, developed a fan base, and told friends about my musical endeavors.

My inconsistency began in the early months of 2021 when I found it difficult to continue producing songs because of other priorities like schooling. I had no external accountability because I was an independent producer without a manager, which resulted in times when I was idle and unproductive. I lost connections as a result of my inconsistent behavior, and both my streams and monthly listener count decreased. Notwithstanding these obstacles, I never completely stopped improving. My career kept advancing, and my statistics never fell to zero.

I didn't let this difficult time stop me from producing lo-fi beats because my music was still being heard by others. Their encouragement inspired me to keep going on, and I never gave up.

I've been devoted to making music ever since, constantly improving my technique. I am self-taught, like many bedroom producers, and I occasionally ask my producer buddies for advice. I also rely on YouTube courses. I have mostly gone it alone throughout my career, navigating the music world on my own and without much help from others. My distinct sound and method of music production have been formed by this journey, which has enabled me to produce genuine and emotional lo-fi hip-hop beats.

My Style of Music

The hip-hop style of lo-fi music (duh) is the style of music I make I mostly create my tunes by scratch using virtual instruments like piano, electric piano, bass guitar, and more. I don't sample music—not the kind that sounds like drum or SFX samples, but the kind that sounds like old records. Making lo-fi music has been simpler because I attempted making trap music back then as well, as trap music is closely related to hip-hop.

I usually try to replicate the vintage feel of the hip-hop scene by incorporating elements of the boom-bap style of hip-hop music into my lo-fi beats. I am still able to produce classic trap music—that is, hip-hop songs that artists used to rap on. I can also create generic house music, which is the kind that has a square wave bass and sounds like deep house. However, I am no longer able to create EDM outside of those.

The truth behind why I quit EDM is because I can't mix very well (I was making music with FL mobile; you know what that means). Unlike in lo-fi music, you have a lot of freedom on how you want your mix to sound like. It doesn't have to sound good, because lo-fi doesn't have to sound clean. The imperfections of lo-fi music are also its beauty.

Note that I'm not that kind of an old soul person. I am a 2003 born kid and yes, I know a lot of old songs in the past, but the kid version of me cared less about who he's listening to.

To be honest, I'm not sure if my beats are considered “lo-fi hip-hop” anymore. For me it's just like generic boom-bap. I don't know. XD
